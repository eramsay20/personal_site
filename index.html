(Analyst) => Developer

A 5-year journey from investment analyst to full-stack software developer

Early Days

After graduating from Bowdoin College with a degree in Economics, I sought opportunities to learn all I could about finance. For my first job out of school I spent a little over a year at one of the most risk averse employers in the US, a Fortune 500 insurance company. After about a year I longed for more of a challenge. Shortly into my search an opportunity came up to take an 80% pay cut and begin life anew as an entry level investment analyst at a top-tier VC firm in Shanghai. I signed right away and moved to China a month later.

Learn more

Life In the Startup World

Needless to say, it was an adjustment! This new role at SOSV, a global leader in startup accelerator programs, gave me precisely what I hoped for. As one of the leading early stage investment firms in the world, I was able to connect with, learn from, coach and fund top tier mobile software startups from around the world looking to break tackle the China market. After 18 months having completed over ~25 deals across Shanghai and Taipei, I found myself itching to join a startup myself. Opportunity came knocking once again in December, 2016, when a close friend found a role at a budding fintech startup called eShares based in San Francisco. This role would give me an opportunity to learn how to value a company and connect 1:1 with its founders to learn how their businesses tick. While hopeful that the company itself would succeed, this next step was exactly what I was looking for. I couldn't have known at the time that this budding startup would one day become a multi-billion dollar unicorn, called Carta... but 5 years later, here we are!

Learn more

Growing Through A Pandemic

After a few years at Carta (formerly eShares), I decided to try and bring some balance to my life and move back closer to family who had all moved to Maryland. At the time, remote work opportunities were still relatively few and far between, so ultimately this move meant leaving Carta. With my equity gains I decided to explore real estate investing, got my license and started to learn the ropes just as COVID-19 decided to turn the world on its head. With time to reflect on where I wanted to head next, opportunity once again opened doors as my wife, May, opted into an unpaid full-stack developer training roll. While she worked from home, I had the benefit of seeing the engineering life in action, and having worked with developers and entrepreneurs for years already, decided to put my skillsets and network to use in a developer roll myself. As I looked into which programs could offer me the most in-depth and comprehensive experience, App Academy stood out amongst its competitors and I join its full-stack software engineering program.
